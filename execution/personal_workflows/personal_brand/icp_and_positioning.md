## How to recognize the ICP — the 3-signal filter

Someone is a real buyer when **2 of these 3** are true:

1. **They name an OUTCOME, not a technology.** "I want more booked meetings" / "I need this
   feature live" — not "I want a RAG pipeline."
2. **They have project budget, but can't justify a full-time hire or a €100k / 6-month agency.**
3. **They have urgency — weeks, not quarters.**

2/3 → engage. 0–1 → politely qualify out.

### Anti-ICP — bin fast (don't spend a minute here)
- Equity-only / "be my technical co-founder."
- $50 gig hunters who want a junior dev by the hour.
- Enterprise procurement, SOC2/vendor-onboarding gauntlets, 3-month legal.
- No budget, no deadline, "just exploring."

| Audience | What keeps them up | The one line that lands | Lead offer |

| **VCs / operators** (not buyers — **referrers**) | A portfolio company needs an AI thing shipped and has no one | "When a portfolio company needs an AI system built fast, I'm who you tag." | Referral: be the trusted builder in their rolodex |
| **Heads of Product / Ops** (scaleups) | Roadmap gap they can't staff | "A senior operator who de-risks delivery because he can both scope it and build it." | Fractional AI builder |

**Resolving the "reach everyone" tension:** the *wedge* is one (PM judgment × builder proof);
the *sentence* changes per audience. The launch post carries all four in one breath; every other
post picks ONE audience and speaks only to them. Generic = ignored; targeted = shared.
